Water Ways of Alappuzha

Natural beuty
Alappuzha, also known as Alleppey, is famous for its extensive network of backwaters and waterways. The city is located in the southern Indian state of Kerala and is often referred to as the "Venice of the East" due to its intricate network of canals, lakes, and lagoons.

Onam Festival

Celebrations
Onam is a traditional harvest festival celebrated in the Indian state of Kerala. It is known for its colorful flower decorations, grand feasts, and traditional dance performances.

Munnar Hill Tops

Natural beuty
Munnar is a picturesque hill station located in the Western Ghats mountain range in the Indian state of Kerala. Known for its lush green tea plantations, misty hills, and cool climate, Munnar is a popular tourist destination.

Thrissur Pooram

Celebrations
Thrissur Pooram is a vibrant temple festival held annually in the city of Thrissur in Kerala, India. It is one of the most famous and spectacular festivals in Kerala and attracts thousands of visitors from all over the world.

Periyar Wild Life Sanctuary

Wild Life
Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary is a renowned wildlife reserve located in the Western Ghats mountain range of Kerala, India. The sanctuary is known for its diverse flora and fauna, including elephants, tigers, gaurs, and various species of birds.

Theyyam

Art form
Theyyam is a traditional ritual art form performed in the northern part of Kerala and some parts of Karnataka, India. It is a vibrant and colorful dance form that incorporates elements of dance, music, and drama.

Bird Sanctuary

Wild life
The Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary, also known as the Vembanad Bird Sanctuary, is a popular bird sanctuary located in the Kottayam district of Kerala, India. It is situated on the banks of Lake Vembanad and covers an area of about 14 acres.

Kadhakali

Art Form
Kadha Kali is a traditional dance form from the state of Kerala in India. It is a colorful and vibrant dance performance that combines elements of dance, drama, music, and martial arts. The performers wear elaborate costumes and use exaggerated gestures and facial expressions to convey the story.

Mattanjeri Palace

Royal History
The Mattancherry Palace, also known as the Dutch Palace, is a historical palace located in Mattancherry, a neighborhood of Kochi (Cochin), in the Indian state of Kerala. The palace was built by the Portuguese in 1555 and later renovated and expanded by the Dutch in 1663.

Boat Race

Sports
The boat race in Kerala, India is a popular and traditional annual event that takes place in the backwaters and rivers of the state. The most famous of these races is the Nehru Trophy Boat Race, which is held on the Punnamada Lake near Alappuzha.
